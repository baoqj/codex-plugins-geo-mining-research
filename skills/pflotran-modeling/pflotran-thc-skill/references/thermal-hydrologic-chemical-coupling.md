# Thermal-Hydrologic-Chemical Coupling

Use THC coupling when temperature materially affects density/viscosity, reaction kinetics, mineral equilibrium, or groundwater chemistry.

Common scenarios:

- geothermal groundwater;
- mine heat pollution;
- nuclear waste heat;
- deep fractured rock;
- thermal stimulation or seasonal thermal storage.

Keep temperature fields and heat boundaries as placeholders unless measured or explicitly scenario-based.
