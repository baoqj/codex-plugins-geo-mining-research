# Example: Petrogenesis and Isotope Geochemistry Paper Architecture

## Input Topic

Use whole-rock geochemistry, zircon U-Pb ages, and Hf-Nd-Sr isotope data to evaluate granitoid petrogenesis and crustal evolution.

## Paper Type Classification

- Primary type: Petrogenesis Paper
- Secondary type: Isotope Geochemistry Paper
- Tertiary type: Regional Geochemical Characterization Paper

## Research Questions

- What magma source and differentiation processes explain the granitoid suite?
- Do isotope and age constraints support juvenile crustal addition, crustal reworking, or mixed sources?
- How much of the geochemical signal may reflect alteration, inheritance, or sampling bias?

## Hypotheses

- Integrated major/trace, REE, and isotope patterns can distinguish source inheritance from fractional crystallization or crustal assimilation.

## Required Data

- Petrography, major oxides, trace elements, REE, zircon ages, Hf-Nd-Sr isotope ratios, analytical uncertainty, standards, alteration indicators.

## Methods

- TAS/QAPF classification.
- Harker trend interpretation.
- REE and primitive mantle normalization.
- Isotope evolution and source comparison.
- Alteration and uncertainty screening.

## Figure Plan

- Geological setting map.
- TAS or QAPF diagram.
- Harker diagrams.
- REE and spider diagrams.
- Epsilon-time isotope plot.
