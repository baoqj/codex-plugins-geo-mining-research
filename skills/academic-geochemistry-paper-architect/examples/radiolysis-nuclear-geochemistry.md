# Example: Porous-Media Radiolysis Nuclear Geochemistry Paper Architecture

## Input Topic

Study how water radiolysis in porous oxide and clay media changes G-values and affects groundwater redox evolution and radionuclide migration.

## Paper Type Classification

- Primary type: Radiolysis / Nuclear Geochemistry Paper
- Secondary type: Reactive Transport Modelling Paper
- Tertiary type: Hydrogeochemistry Paper

## Research Questions

- How do pore confinement, mineral surfaces, dose rate, and groundwater chemistry alter radiolytic yields?
- How do radiolysis products affect redox-sensitive radionuclide speciation and mobility?
- Which assumptions prevent extrapolation from bulk-water radiolysis to porous geological media?

## Hypotheses

- Porous media modify radical recombination and reactive-species lifetimes relative to bulk water.
- Redox changes driven by radiolysis can alter radionuclide speciation only within explicit dose-rate, transport, and reaction-boundary limits.

## Required Data

- Radiation field, dose rate, LET if available, pore geometry, mineral surface area, groundwater chemistry, temperature, reaction yields, radionuclide species.

## Methods

- Reaction network architecture.
- G-value comparison and uncertainty audit.
- Redox/speciation modelling plan.
- Boundary-condition and safety-caveat statement.

## Figure Plan

- Radiolysis reaction network.
- Bulk water versus porous water G-value comparison.
- Pore-size or surface-area effect plot.
- Redox and radionuclide speciation conceptual model.
