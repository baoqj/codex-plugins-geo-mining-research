# Fracture Aperture Evolution

Fracture aperture may evolve due to:

- Effective stress change.
- Shear or normal deformation.
- Thermal stress.
- Mineral precipitation.
- Mineral dissolution.
- Swelling or clay alteration.
- Damage growth.

Model choices:

- Constant aperture.
- Stress-dependent aperture.
- Mineral-volume-dependent aperture.
- Coupled mechanical-chemical aperture.

Do not include fracture aperture feedback without evidence that it materially affects flow or transport.
