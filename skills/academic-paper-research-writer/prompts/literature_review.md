Construct a literature review plan for the research topic.

The review must be analytical, not a list of papers.

Return:
1. Key research clusters;
2. Seminal papers and why they matter;
3. Recent papers and what they add;
4. Competing explanations;
5. Methodological differences among studies;
6. Known data gaps;
7. Conflicting evidence;
8. How the current paper positions itself;
9. Which claims require citations;
10. Suggested citation style.

For each literature cluster, include:
- research question addressed;
- methods used;
- main findings;
- limitation;
- relevance to the current paper.

Rules:
- Do not summarize papers mechanically.
- Compare mechanisms, assumptions, methods, and scales.
- Highlight what remains unresolved.
- Mark any claim that requires source verification.
