Perform a peer-review-style quality check on the generated paper.

Evaluate:
1. Is the research question specific enough?
2. Is the novelty clear?
3. Are the claims supported by evidence?
4. Are equations correct, necessary, and fully defined?
5. Are units consistent?
6. Are boundary conditions stated?
7. Are assumptions explicit?
8. Are alternative explanations considered?
9. Are limitations honestly stated?
10. Is the citation style consistent?
11. Does the paper overclaim industrial feasibility?
12. Does it distinguish mechanism from application?
13. Does it include enough quantitative reasoning?
14. Is the abstract aligned with the actual paper?
15. Is the conclusion supported by the preceding analysis?

Return:
- Major issues;
- Minor issues;
- Missing evidence;
- Suggested revisions;
- Publication readiness score from 1 to 5.
