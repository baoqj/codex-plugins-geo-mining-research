Build an evidence matrix for the paper.

For each major claim, return:
1. Claim;
2. Evidence type: literature, equation, dataset, simulation, experiment, or inference;
3. Supporting sources or required sources;
4. Confidence level;
5. Known uncertainty;
6. Alternative explanations;
7. What additional evidence would strengthen the claim.

Rules:
- Do not treat all evidence as equal.
- Separate directly observed results from inferred mechanisms.
- Identify where the paper relies on analogy from related systems.
- Identify claims that are currently too speculative.
- For industrial feasibility claims, require energy balance and cost boundary.
