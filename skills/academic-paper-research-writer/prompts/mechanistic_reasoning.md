Develop the mechanistic framework for the paper.

Return:
1. System description;
2. Main species or state variables;
3. Primary processes;
4. Secondary processes;
5. Interface or surface reactions;
6. Transport mechanisms;
7. Energy sources;
8. Feedback mechanisms;
9. Expected direction of change;
10. Boundary conditions;
11. Conditions under which the conclusion may fail.

For porous media radiolysis, explicitly analyze:
- ionizing radiation energy deposition;
- hydrated electron formation;
- hydroxyl radical formation;
- hydrogen and hydrogen peroxide generation;
- electron-hole separation;
- geminate recombination;
- spur expansion;
- pore confinement;
- surface scavenging;
- oxide-water band alignment;
- metal surface electron capture;
- electric double layer effects;
- pH and dissolved oxygen effects;
- dose rate and LET effects.

Rules:
- Always distinguish between bulk water and confined water.
- Always specify whether a mechanism increases or decreases a given G-value.
- Always state the conditions under which the mechanism is expected to dominate.
