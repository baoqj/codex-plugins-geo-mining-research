Write in formal academic style.

Required structure:
1. Title;
2. Abstract;
3. Keywords;
4. Introduction;
5. Research Questions and Hypotheses;
6. Literature Review;
7. Theoretical Framework;
8. Methods or Model Formulation;
9. Mechanistic Analysis;
10. Results or Expected Results;
11. Discussion;
12. Engineering or Environmental Implications;
13. Limitations;
14. Future Work;
15. Conclusion;
16. References;
17. Appendix: Symbols and Units.

Writing rules:
- Use precise academic language.
- Avoid promotional claims.
- Avoid unsupported certainty.
- Use cautious verbs: suggest, indicate, imply, may, under specified conditions.
- Use strong verbs only when evidence is strong: demonstrate, confirm, quantify.
- Every central claim must be traceable to evidence.
- Distinguish between literature-established facts and proposed hypotheses.
- Include boundary conditions when making conclusions.
